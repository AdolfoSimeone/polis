POLIS

One person sending an email to 500 people works just fine. Low complexity, high comprehension. 500 people hitting the reply button? Input overload - high complexity, low comprehension. This same problem crops up in all manner of human communication - education, internal business communications & market research, politics, professional communities & conferences, families, etc. Polis is a communication structure made of beautiful math that leverages crowdsourcing and data visualization to make largescale conversations possible. What's that mean? It means you can feel free to think big with your communications. Go ahead, ask your town something, Mr. Mayor. Rather than a flood of letters and calls, Polis redirects messages back to users who will agree or disagree with their peers, then cluster based on their responses. You'll receive one neat package clearly summarizing the main groups and the ideas that brought them together. Awesome. But Polis isn't just for individuals, really, at its core, Polis is about groups. It's hard to get large groups to talk productively about complex topics. Polis provides a platform that helps decentralized groups:

  1. emerge and distinguish themselves from other groups
  2. synthesize their ideas
  3. define their values and beliefs in meaningful and enduring ways
  4. identify the factual basis for those values and beliefs
  5. communicate with other groups
  6. propose and mobilize actions

Polisapp.org accomplishes this by:

  1. curating credible resources and diverse opinions
  2. restricting users from directly communicating with one another
  3. leveraging real-time data visualization to illuminate groups and patterns

Polis is composed of a small, professional team of web developers and data scientists. We are driven by a deep belief that ideas are important and that novel technological structures have the power to impose lasting changes on human behavior. Data visualization, machine learning, crowdsourcing, human computer symbiosis & distributed computing get us excited.
